Research

My dissertation develops algorithms that attribute causal responsibility in high-dimensional multi-omics data — distinguishing direct perturbation effects from those mediated by shifts in gut microbial composition. The work combines novel statistical methods, consumer-resource ecological modeling, and experimental validation.

CompoRes — Compositional Regression with Bootstrapped Significance

A key challenge in host–microbiome research is that when a patient receives a drug, both host physiology and gut microbiota change simultaneously. CompoRes is a four-step algorithmic framework that resolves this:

  1. Infers true taxon correlations from compositional (relative abundance) data via SparCC-based estimation, bypassing spurious correlations inherent to sequencing.
  2. Hierarchically clusters amplicon sequence variants into Operational Compositional Units (OCUs) that capture co-varying functional groups.
  3. Computes log-ratio regressions between all OCU pairs and host gene expression profiles in Aitchison geometry.
  4. Estimates statistical significance via bootstrapped data shuffling, producing per-gene p-values that distinguish true biological correlations from noise.

CompoRes was validated on a comprehensive antibiotic perturbation resource in SPF mice (four antibiotics × three administration routes), coupled with 16S rRNA sequencing, shotgun metagenomics, and colonic RNA-seq. The tool correctly predicts germ-free mouse outcomes using only SPF data (AUROC >0.9), and performs equivalently with 16S and metagenomic inputs (Pearson r=0.579, p<10−100).

A key biological discovery: systemic vancomycin disrupts diurnal gene expression in the gut through a microbiota-independent, neurally mediated mechanism — inducing Neuromedin U (Nmu) expression, reducing food intake, and abolishing diurnal MHC class II expression. This was confirmed in Nmu−/− mice and conserved in Drosophila.

CorrEnrich — Correlation-Aware Pathway Enrichment

Standard GSEA identifies pathways containing differentially expressed genes but does not verify their co-regulation. CorrEnrich selects Gene Ontology clusters whose constituent genes exhibit significant expression correlation across samples within a condition, filtering out pathways enriched by chance. Applied throughout the antibiotic resource study, it identifies unique transcriptional signatures of individual antibiotics and their non-additive interactions.

Mathematical Validation via Consumer-Resource Models

To benchmark CompoRes rigorously, I am constructing a synthetic framework using continuous consumer-resource chemostat models. By applying the Implicit Function Theorem to steady-state equations, the system's full sensitivity matrix under perturbation can be derived analytically, providing a mathematical ground truth. Preliminary simulations (10,000 synthetic communities, 3 species, 4 nutrients) confirm that CompoRes recovers the analytically predicted interaction structure.

Applications

Gut–Brain Axis in Aged Stroke (collaboration with Blacher Lab, HUJI) — Multi-omics integration of scRNA-seq (~20,000 cells), targeted LC-MS/MS lipidomics, and 16S sequencing from aged and young stroke mice. CompoRes identified that aged stroke mice fail to produce key anti-inflammatory lipid metabolites (HYA and KetoD) due to depletion of specific gut taxa (Lachnospiraceae), mechanistically linking dysbiosis to gut barrier failure and neuroinflammation.

Clinical Diagnostics from Archived Biopsies — Crohn’s Disease (funded by Kenneth Rainin Foundation) — Applying the pipeline to archived FFPE intestinal biopsies linked to multi-decade longitudinal records. The pipeline integrates 16S V3–V4 sequencing from FFPE tissue, high-plex spatial imaging (70+ antibody markers + bacterial FISH probes via Akoya PhenoCycler), and machine learning classifiers for risk stratification of newly diagnosed IBD patients.
