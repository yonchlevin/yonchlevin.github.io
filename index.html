About

I am a PhD candidate in the Direct PhD track in Physics at the Racah Institute of Physics, Hebrew University of Jerusalem (2022–present). My research sits at the intersection of computational biology and biophysics, focusing on how the gut microbiome and host interact — and how antibiotics perturb that relationship.

I completed my B.Sc. in Physics and Computer Science at the Hebrew University (2019–2022, GPA 93.3), as part of the Amirim science excellence program. Before my graduate studies, I served as a Mechanical Designer in Unit 8200 of the Israeli military (2016–2019).

I am a recipient of the prestigious Milner Scholarship (2025–2029) for outstanding PhD students, as well as the HUJI CIDR Scholarship for excellent data science research.

Research

My work uses computational and statistical tools to understand complex biological systems, with a focus on:

  • Host–microbiome interactions — modeling how gut bacteria and host physiology influence each other under normal conditions and antibiotic perturbation.
  • Multi-omics data integration — combining transcriptomic, metagenomic, and metabolomic datasets using methods such as DIABLO, ANCOM-BC, Random Forest, and Information Bottleneck theory.
  • Consumer-resource models — ecological modeling of microbial communities.
  • Quantum computation (earlier work) — protocols for cluster state generation with quantum metasurfaces.

Skills: Python · R · MATLAB · C · C++
